SLE — Single Loss Expectancy

What one occurrence of the threat costs you.

ARO — Annualised Rate of Occurrence

How many times a year you expect it to happen. 0.1 means once a decade.

ALE — Annualised Loss Expectancy

ALE = SLE × ARO. A control is worth buying only when it costs less than the ALE it removes.

Change any figure to explore. A control is marked justified when its yearly cost is less than the annual loss (ALE) it would prevent — the core cost–benefit test of risk management.
